Paris, France

The City of Light

About Paris

Paris, France's capital, is a major European city and a global center for art, fashion, gastronomy and culture. Its picturesque 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by wide boulevards and the River Seine, with landmarks like the Eiffel Tower and Gothic Notre-Dame cathedral.

Top Attractions

Eiffel Tower

The iconic iron tower built by Gustave Eiffel for the 1889 World's Fair. At 324 meters tall, it offers stunning views of the city from its three observation decks. At night, the tower sparkles with thousands of lights for five minutes every hour.

Louvre Museum

The world's largest art museum and a historic monument in Paris. Home to thousands of works from antiquity to the 19th century, including Leonardo da Vinci's Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo sculpture.

Notre-Dame Cathedral

A masterpiece of French Gothic architecture, famous for its size, antiquity, and architectural interest. Construction began in 1163 and was largely completed by 1260. The cathedral is currently undergoing restoration after the 2019 fire.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

April-June and September-October offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds. July-August is peak season with warm weather but larger crowds.

Getting Around

Paris has an excellent metro system. Consider purchasing a Paris Visite travel card or a carnet of 10 single tickets for convenience.
