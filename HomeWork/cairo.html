Cairo, Egypt

Gateway to the Pyramids

About Cairo

Cairo, Egypt's sprawling capital, is set on the Nile River. At its heart is Tahrir Square and the vast Egyptian Museum, a trove of antiquities including royal mummies and gilded King Tutankhamun artifacts. Nearby, Giza is the site of the iconic pyramids and Great Sphinx, dating to the 26th century BC.

Top Attractions

Giza Pyramids & Sphinx

The last surviving wonder of the ancient world, the Pyramids of Giza were built as tombs for pharaohs around 2500 BC. The Great Pyramid of Khufu is the largest, originally standing 146 meters tall. The nearby Great Sphinx has the body of a lion and the head of a pharaoh.

Egyptian Museum

Home to the world's most extensive collection of pharaonic antiquities, including the treasures of Tutankhamun's tomb. The museum houses over 120,000 items, with the highlights being the gold mask of Tutankhamun and the Royal Mummy Room.

Khan el-Khalili Bazaar

A major souk in the historic center of Islamic Cairo dating back to 1382. The labyrinthine market is filled with shops selling jewelry, spices, perfumes, and traditional crafts. It's a great place to experience local culture and practice your bargaining skills.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

October-April offers pleasant temperatures. December and January are peak season. Summers (May-September) can be extremely hot.

Getting Around

Taxis and ride-hailing apps are the most convenient way to get around. The metro is efficient but limited in coverage. Organized tours are recommended for visiting the pyramids.
